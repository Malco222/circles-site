Privacy

Effective: on publish · Plain words, no legalese

Circles is built on one principle: your relationships are yours. Here is exactly what the app does with your information.

  • Everything you put into Circles — people, notes, moments, settings — lives on your device. No account, no sign-up, no Circles server storing your data.
  • We collect nothing. No analytics, no ad identifiers, no tracking.
  • The few features that touch the network are optional, obvious, and under your control — all of them are listed below.

What stays on your device

Your people directory, notes, logged moments, open loops, circles, cadences, photos, and settings are stored only in the app's private storage on your device — the app's automatic restore points too. We cannot see, read, or recover any of it. That is a design decision, not a limitation.

Because your data lives on your device, it is included in your normal iCloud or computer backup if you have that enabled — governed by Apple's privacy terms, not ours.

The optional features that use the network

Calendar (optional). If you paste a calendar (.ics) link, the app fetches that calendar to show who you're seeing and quietly log past meetings. Its contents are processed and stored on your device. Remove the link in Settings to stop all calendar fetching.

AI note sorting (optional). When enabled, the text of a note you're parsing — plus the names and locations in your directory, so the AI can match the right person — is sent to the AI provider (Anthropic) for processing, and is not stored by Circles anywhere off your device. You always review the result before anything saves. Turn it off in Settings → Privacy & Security and notes are sorted entirely on your device.

AI message drafts (optional). Asking for a draft sends that person's context from your notes to the AI provider once, to write the draft. You edit and send it yourself — Circles never sends messages on your behalf.

That is the complete list. If a feature is not named above, it does not use the network.

Face ID and passcode

The optional app lock uses Face ID / Touch ID through Apple's system APIs. Your biometric data never leaves your device's secure hardware and is never available to Circles. The fallback passcode is stored only as a salted cryptographic hash, on your device.

Notifications

All reminders — birthdays, nudges, pre-meeting briefs — are generated and scheduled on your device. There is no push server.

Your data, your exit

Export everything as a readable file anytime. Back up to your own iCloud Drive with one tap — storage you control, under your Apple ID. Delete everything with Settings → Clear all data. Because we hold nothing, there is nothing else to ask us to delete.

Children

Circles is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from anyone — including children.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the new version will be posted here with a new effective date. Because the architecture is local-first, a change that sent your data anywhere new would be a major, explicit product change — not a quiet policy edit.
